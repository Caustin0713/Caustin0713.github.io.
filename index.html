Leopard Gecko

About Leopard Geckos

The Leopard Gecko ( Eublepharis macularius ) Leopard geckos are popular, hardy lizard's native to the deserts of Asia, known for their docile nature, relatively easy care, and long lifespans in captivity, making them excellent beginner reptiles. .

There are many subspecies within the leopard gecko species.

Species

  • Afghan Leopard Gecko
  • East Indian Leopard Gecko
  • Eublepharis macularius fasciolatus

Features

Leopard geckos are mostly nocturnal, ground-dwelling geckos that are generally docile and easy to tame. They do not have sticky toe pads like other geckos, so they do not climb walls. Also, unlike other geckos, they do have eyelids.

Diet

What is best to feed Leopard Geckos

  1. Crickets
  2. Dubai Roaches
  3. Mealworms

Ideal tank set up for Leopard Gecko

For a beginner leopard gecko tank setup, you'll need a 20-gallon long tank, a heat mat (covering 30% of the enclosure), a thermostat, a thermometer, a hydrometer, a substrate like reptile carpet or paper towels, at least two hides, a moist hide, and a water dish.
